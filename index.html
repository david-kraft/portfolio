About Me

Biography

I am passionate about serving the communities I identify with. These include the local Native American and Ojibwe communities, the disabled and autistic community, as well as the communities where I have lived, worked, served, or volunteered in.

In my 30+ years of life, I make connections—In person, in writing, and on the web. I use these connections both to teach and learn from the same people, whether they are students, clients, employers, colleagues, friends, family or anyone else. I desire a job where I can honestly tell the world that I live to work, as opposed to a job where I just work to live, though I also need to make enough to live if I am to sustain my place in your workplace community.

Coding Boot Camp

I am in the final weeks of a full-stack web development boot camp, where I am learning to code websites myself instead of using Wordpress, Drupal, or Wix to put one together. Technologies I feel confident using are HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap 4, JavaScript, ES6, JSX, MySQL, Git, GitHub, Heroku, making API calls, and jQuery. I also have been exposed to React.js, Moment.js, MongoDB, Mongoose, and Sequelize.

Initially, I was taking this course because I thought my place in life was to be a web developer, because I felt I was better with computers than I am with people. Since then, I have learned nothing could be further from the truth. I would collapse without human interaction. Even if it means making five figures instead of six for the rest of my life, I need to be happy and my jobs need to be sustainable and feel like home to me.

AmeriCorps VISTA service at MIGIZI

I have always volunteered doing something, whether it be working with kids at a hospital or tutoring immigrants at the library. It seemed natural for me to serve my community through AmeriCorps. I am a member of an Ojibwe tribe in Wisconsin, but I really didn't know very much about my Native heritage.

MIGIZI, which is the Ojibwe term for Bald Eagle and I had a symbiotic relationship while I worked there. I gave them my technological expertise, my time, and my teaching skills, and they both taught me as much as they teach the youth they teach and they connected me with elders. Now I am participating in religious ceremonies and investing permanently as part of their community.

The Minnesota Council of Nonprofits was the sponsoring organization of my VISTA project. They brought me to their conferences on fundraising, communication, leadership, and finances. Overall, I was eager to repeat the experience once my first year was ended.

BA in Communication Studies from University of Minnesota

Despite it being a single department, Communication Studies at the UofM is actually comprised of three very different fields: Interpersonal Communication, Rhetorical Studies, and Media Studies. Honestly, I never had much interest in studying media. While I was also highly intrigued by rhetorical studies and the mechanics of persuasive speech, interpersonal communication was my major focus. That is because I am autistic and wanted to find ways to grow more effective and increase my competency as a listener and communicator both for personal and professional reasons.

One of my greatest strengths and challenges is that it is hard for me to accept that I am limited and constrained by the boundaries from my autistic identity. Whenever I find my efforts are insufficient, I learn and grow smarter and more capable so the next time I face a challenge, I succeed. Fun fact: I took Latin as my second language. I studied Cicero and Caesar's prose as well as Vergil's poetry. I also challenged myself with as many graduate level courses as I could. Sometimes I ended up dropping when I took on too much, but many classes, like Advanced Theories in Interpersonal Communication, Conflict and Communication, and Major Rhetorical Theories of the 20th Century, in which I was the only undergraduate student. I also participated in a faculty member's research project and conducted my own independent study for which I earned an A.

One of the things I miss most about the University of Minnesota was the library system. My ability to research anything that interested me was beyond compare. When I was there I learned not only advanced search methods and how to find relevant information in scholarly articles, I also learned how to use citation managers like Zotero and RefWorks to save my sources in a database in my browser or in the cloud and automatically generate bibliographies in Word. Most students I've worked with know about EasyBib and other basic tools and I show them Zotero and how it rocked my own world and they have all been impressed.

I also worked for a short time in the libraries at the University of Minnesota Archives where I retrieved materials and digitized records for the University and their customers.
